Stroke Prevention


Although there are effective treatments for strokes, the most effective treatment is to take preventative measures to ensure your risk level remains low. There are many ways to reduce your chance of having a stroke, and also to improve your overall health.

  • Lower your blood pressure. High blood pressure increases your risk of a stroke enormously, so reducing it should be a high priority. Performing the following steps is very helpful.
    1. 1. Avoid high cholesterol foods
    2. 2. Eat healthier. Try to include fruit or vegetables in every meal.
    3. 3. Make sure you get at least 30 minutes of exercise per day.
  • Lose weight. A body weight above the health range not only increases your risk of stroke, but is also linked to high blood pressure. You should aim to have a BMI (body mass index) of no more than 25. By ensuring that you have a healthy diet and exercise each day you should be able to lose weight.
  • Drink in moderation. Believe it or not, drinking can actually reduce your chance of having a stroke. However, this is only in moderation. By drinking one glass per day you can actually reduce your risk, but any more will lead to an increase.
  • Look after your health problems. Diseases and illnesses such as diabetes and irregular heartbeats are indicators of an increased risk of stroke. By monitoring these illnesses, you can keep your chance of having a stroke to a minimum.
  • Improve your overall bodily health. A stroke is much less likely to occur in a person who follows these steps:
    1. 1. No smoking, or quit smoking as soon as possible. Smoking thickens your blood, which increases the chances of a clot forming in your brain. Even if you are a smoker, quitting now ill still reduce your chance of having a stroke significantly.
    2. 2. Exercise more. Exercise helps reduce a lot of stroke factors, but is also good at reducing strokes by itself. All adults should aim to exercise to at least moderate intensity (i.e you are breathing hard, but you can still talk) at least five days a week.

If you follow all the steps on this list, not only will your overall health improve, but your risk of suffering a stroke will decrease significantly.
