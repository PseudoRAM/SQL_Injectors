Stroke Treatments

Once a stroke has been diagnosed in a patient, treatment will often be administered as quickly as possible, in order to maximise the chance of survival. There various different treatments available, ranging from surgical procedures to taking medical drugs to help treat a patient. The treatment they will recieve will also be dependent on what type of stroke they've had (Ishcemic or Hemorhaggic).

ISCHEMIC STROKE TREATMENTS


The first treatment available for Ischemic Strokes is a drug called tPA, which is administered to the brain's blood flow. tPA is a tissue plasminogen activator, which means that it is able to dissolve the clot in the brain and restore blood flow to it. If this treatment is administered to the stroke victim within three hours, it can significantly improve the patients chance of recovery.


The second treatment available for Ischemic Strokes is a procedure known as Mechanical Thrombectomy. This treatment is only available after the patient has recieved their tPA injection. In this operation, a catheter is threaded through an artery in the groin all the way ip to the blood clot in the brain. Suction tubes are then used to assist doctors in removing the clot so that blood flow may return to normal. For this treatment to be effictive, it must be performed within six hours of the stroke occuring.


HEMORHAGGIC STROKE TREATMENTS


The first treatment that is available for Hemorhaggic Stroke patients is called an Endovascular Procedure. This procedure is similar to the mechanical thrombectomy. A catheter is inserted into an artery in the arm or leg, and then guided to the brain. A mechanical agent such as a coil is then used to prevent any further damage to the brain's blood supply.


The second treatment available is a surgical one. The operation is done to stop blood from bleeding onto the brain. There are various methods used during this operation, but the most common one is using a metal clip. For example, in the case of a ruptured aneurysm, the metal clip is attached to the base of the aneurysm to prevent any further bleeding or damage.
