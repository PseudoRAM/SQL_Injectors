WHAT IS A STROKE?

A stroke is often reffered to as a brain attack. It happens when the blood flow to the brain is interrupted, whether it be from an artery bursting (A Hemorrhagic Stroke), or from an artery becoming blocked (An Ischemic Stroke). When these occur the fresh supply of blood and oxygen from the heart and lungs is cut off, causing the brain cells to become deprived of oxygen and die. When this happens, various things can occur depending on which part of the brain is affected, such as muscle and memory loss.


TYPES OF STROKES

HEMORRHAGIC STROKE

Hemorrhagic strokes are the least common of the two, only around 20% of strokes are Hemorrhagic, however, they still account for about 40% of of all stroke related deaths. A Hemorrhagic stroke occurs from either an aneurysm burst, or from a blood vessel leaking. Swelling occurs and pressure is formed around the brain as the blood surrounds it, ultimately damaging both the cells and tissue of the brain. Read more here.

ISCHEMIC STROKE

Ischemic strokes are caused when an artery to the brain is blocked off, causing a loss of blood to certain areas of the brain. It is the most common type of stroke, accounting for up 80% of strokes and 60% of stroke related deaths. As the brain relies on the arteries to bring fresh blood and oxygen form the heart and lungs, the brain cells will not be able to function if they are cut off from this supply, as they won't be able to make enough energy.
