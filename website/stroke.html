WHAT IS A STROKE?

A stroke is often reffered to as a brain attack. It happens when the blood flow to the brain is interrupted, whether it be from an artery bursting (A Hemorrhagic Stroke), or from an artery becoming blocked (An Ischemic Stroke). When these occur the fresh supply of blood and oxygen from the heart and lungs is cut off, causing the brain cells to become deprived of oxygen and die. When this happens, various things can occur depending on which part of the brain is affected, such as muscle and memory loss.


HOW TO IDENTIFY A STROKE



SYMPTOMS

There are a number of symptoms that a stroke can have. The most common are arranged into the acronym FAST. If you notice a person suffering from any of these symptoms, take note of the time of occurence and call 000 immediately.


  • F - Face. Check their face. See if it is drooping and if they can smile.
  • A - Arms. Check if they are experiencing weakness in their arms. Can they lift them?
  • S - Speech. Is their speech slurred? Can they understand you?
  • T - Time. If you observe any of these symptoms, call 000 immediately

HOW YOU CAN HELP

If you notice anyone displaying any of the symptoms above, ensure you call 000 immediately. While you are waiting for an ambulance to arrive, you can help by doing the following:

  • If the person is conscious, lay them on their side with their head raised.
  • Ensure that no one gives them anything to eat or drink.
  • Make sure to loosen any restrictive clothing to ensure proper blood flow.
  • If they are unconcious and aren't breathing or are without a pulse, start CPR immediately.
  • A call taker at 000 can provide CPR instructions over the phone.

Even if you slighly suspect that someone may be experiencing a stroke, call 000 immediately.
