TYPES OF STROKE

There are two different types of strokes: Hemorrhagic and Ischemic. A Hemorrhagic stroke is least common and is usually caused from blood leaking onto the brain. An Ischemic stroke is the most common variant and is often caused from a blocked artery or blood vessel in the brain.

HEMORRHAGIC STROKE

Hemorrhagic strokes are the least common of the two, only around 20% of strokes are Hemorrhagic, however, they still account for about 40% of of all stroke related deaths. A Hemorrhagic stroke occurs from either an aneurysm burst, or from a blood vessel leaking. Swelling occurs and pressure is formed around the brain as the blood surrounds it, ultimately damaging both the cells and tissue of the brain.

ISCHEMIC STROKE

Ischemic strokes are caused when an artery to the brain is blocked off, causing a loss of blood to certain areas of the brain. It is the most common type of stroke, accounting for up 80% of strokes and 60% of stroke related deaths. As the brain relies on the arteries to bring fresh blood and oxygen form the heart and lungs, the brain cells will not be able to function if they are cut off from this supply, as they won't be able to make enough energy.
